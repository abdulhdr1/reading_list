Sahil Lavingia (founder of Gumroad) documents his time as a $0-salary software engineer at the Department of Veterans Affairs under the DOGE (Department of Government Efficiency) initiative. He joined in March 2025, got fingerprinted at the VA Medical Center in Manhattan, and arrived at VA Central Office in DC as Senior Advisor to the Chief of Staff.

The post is a day-by-day account of trying to bring startup engineering culture to the federal government ‚Äî an organization with a $350 billion annual impact and 473,000 employees. His first tasks involved reviewing contracts, and the post reveals the enormous gap between how tech startups operate and how government institutions function.

What makes this compelling is the contrast: Lavingia went from running a nimble internet company to navigating federal bureaucracy, motivated by genuine civic duty. He'd canvassed for Bernie Sanders in 2016 and always wanted to write code for the government. The post humanizes the often-politicized DOGE initiative by focusing on the day-to-day reality of trying to improve government systems from the inside.
