Nikunj Kothari argues that clarity of thought and precise instruction is going to matter a LOT more in the near future. This connects directly to the 'context engineering' theme: as AI tools become more capable, the bottleneck shifts from execution to specification.

When you can delegate execution to an AI agent, the quality of your output depends entirely on how clearly you can articulate what you want. Vague instructions produce vague results. Precise, well-structured instructions produce excellent results. This means the most valuable skill isn't coding or design or writing ‚Äî it's thinking clearly enough to give precise instructions.

The implication for education and career development is significant: we need to teach and develop clarity of thought as a primary skill. This was always valuable, but in a world where AI handles execution, it becomes the differentiating capability. People who can clearly define problems, specify requirements, and articulate constraints will massively outperform those who can only do the manual work.
