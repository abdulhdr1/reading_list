Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, lays out three observations about the economics of AI as systems pointing toward AGI come into view. First: the intelligence of an AI model roughly equals the log of resources used to train and run it. You can spend arbitrary amounts and get continuous, predictable gains ‚Äî the scaling laws are accurate over many orders of magnitude.

Second: the cost to use a given level of AI falls about 10x every 12 months. GPT-4's token price dropped roughly 150x from early 2023 to mid-2024 with GPT-4o. Moore's law changed the world at 2x every 18 months; this is dramatically stronger. Third: the socioeconomic value of AI improvements grows superlinearly ‚Äî each incremental improvement unlocks disproportionately more value.

Altman's framing of AGI as 'just another tool in the scaffolding of human progress' alongside electricity, transistors, computers, and the internet, while simultaneously acknowledging 'this time it's different,' captures the tension at the heart of the AI moment. The economic growth ahead looks 'astonishing.' In a decade, perhaps everyone on earth could accomplish more than today's most impactful person.
