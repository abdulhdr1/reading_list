Lucas Crespo argues that most AI design tools fundamentally misunderstand how designers actually work. The post includes visuals illustrating the gap between what AI tools assume designers need and what designers actually do in practice.

The core critique: AI design tools tend to treat design as a generation problem (give me a prompt, get a design) when it's actually an iteration problem (start rough, refine continuously, respond to constraints). Designers don't work by describing what they want in words ‚Äî they work by pushing pixels, testing variations, and developing visual intuition through direct manipulation.

This mirrors the broader 'chat interface for everything' problem: assuming natural language is the best interface for all tasks. For design work specifically, the spatial, visual, and gestural aspects of the creative process are poorly served by text prompts. The best AI design tools will augment the designer's existing workflow (suggesting variations, handling repetitive tasks, generating assets) rather than trying to replace the hands-on creative process.
