Kepano (creator of Obsidian) shares his note-taking workflow: using Obsidian's unique note hotkey to write individual thoughts as they come up throughout the day. Each note is automatically created with a timestamp prefix (YYYY-MM-DD HHmm) and optionally a descriptive title.

The 'fractal' aspect: every few days, he reviews recent notes and connects or expands them. The 'random revisit' aspect: periodically revisiting old notes surfaces forgotten ideas and creates unexpected connections. This creates a self-reinforcing system where past thinking enriches present work.

The approach is notable for its simplicity compared to elaborate knowledge management systems. There's no complex tagging taxonomy, no rigid folder structure, no prerequisite organization. Just capture thoughts as they happen with timestamps, review periodically, and let connections emerge organically. It's the digital equivalent of a researcher's lab notebook ‚Äî low friction, high fidelity, with serendipity built into the review process.
