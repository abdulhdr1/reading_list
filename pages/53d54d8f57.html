Andy Matuschak articulates the value of 'working with the garage door up' ‚Äî a phrase from Robin Sloan. It's the opposite of the Twitter account that only posts announcements of finished work. Instead, it's Screenshot Saturday, it's giving a lecture about problems you're pondering in the shower, it's thinking out loud about why your project doesn't work.

Matuschak loves this kind of communication personally but suspects it also creates more invested, interesting followings over long term. People who follow your process become more deeply engaged than those who only see polished outputs. This connects to his note on anti-marketing and niche projects attracting serendipitous inbounds.

The Robin Sloan passage that inspired it is beautiful: a physical business, by simply existing and having a nice sign, makes the statement 'I am here, working.' There's a scientific glassblowing studio he walks past ‚Äî just seeing someone at work through the window is compelling. The digital equivalent is sharing your process openly: the messy drafts, the failed experiments, the half-formed ideas. Maggie Appleton adds that learning in public gets you invited to cool events with high-achieving people, 'even though you have no right to be there.'
