JungleSilicon asks an intriguing question: is anyone making 'conceptual' L-systems based on semantics or embedding spaces? L-systems (Lindenmayer systems) are formal grammars used to model plant growth and fractal structures ‚Äî traditionally operating on symbolic rules that produce visual patterns.

The idea of applying L-systems to semantic/embedding spaces is fascinating: instead of rules like 'F ‚Üí F[+F]F[-F]F' generating visual branches, imagine rules operating in concept space, where each 'growth step' follows semantic relationships rather than geometric ones. A concept could 'branch' into related ideas, 'grow' toward more specific sub-concepts, or 'flower' into concrete applications.

This sits at the intersection of generative art, computational linguistics, and AI. With modern embedding models that can represent any concept as a vector, the building blocks exist to create such systems. It could produce visual art where the structure reflects semantic relationships, or generate knowledge maps that grow organically like plants. A genuinely novel idea that bridges mathematical formalism and AI.
