Guillermo Rauch reveals that a huge percentage of his feedback to early-stage founders boils down to one thing: remove the friction between the user and the creation of the core 'entity' or 'resource' your company facilitates ‚Äî be it a search query, image generation, document, or deployment.

The principle is powerful in its simplicity: identify what your product creates (the core entity), then ruthlessly eliminate every step between the user's intent and that creation. If you're a document tool, how fast can someone go from opening the app to having a useful document? If you're a deployment platform, how fast from code to live?

This advice is especially relevant in the AI era where the 'core entity' for many products is changing. Products that used to facilitate document creation now facilitate AI-assisted workflows. The friction to remove isn't just UI clicks ‚Äî it's the cognitive overhead of understanding what the product can do and how to make it do it. Rauch's feedback framework is a useful lens for any founder: what is your core entity, and what friction stands between users and creating it?
